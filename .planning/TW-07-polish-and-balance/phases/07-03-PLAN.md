# 07-03 — Combat Narrative

## Objective
Add storytelling to cloud combat results so fights feel dramatic, not just mathematical.

## Context
- See `../BRIEF.md`
- See `../ROADMAP.md`
- Currently: combat returns `{ won, fled, bribed, destroyed, playerHullRemaining, creditsGained }`
- No story text — just stats

## Tasks
1. Add narrative generation to `cloud/src/routes/action.ts` `handleCombat()`
2. Generate strings based on:
   - Action chosen (attack/flee/bribe)
   - Outcome (won/fled/bribed/destroyed)
   - Enemy type (raider/patrol/trader)
   - Damage dealt/taken
3. Examples:
   - Attack + won: "Your plasma cannons tear through their shields. They break apart in a shower of debris. +250 cr looted."
   - Flee + succeeded: "You fire retros and slingshot around an asteroid. They can't match your turn rate."
   - Flee + failed: "You punch the throttle but they anticipated the move. A blast catches your port flank. Hull down to 45."
   - Bribe: "You transfer 500 credits to their account. They power down weapons and let you pass."
4. Return `narrative` field in the combat response
5. Display narrative prominently in CloudSectorScreen combat result

## Verification
- [ ] Attack and win — see victory narrative
- [ ] Attack and lose — see destruction or near-death narrative
- [ ] Flee successfully — see escape narrative
- [ ] Flee and fail — see damage narrative
- [ ] Bribe — see payoff narrative

## Done When
- Every combat outcome has a narrative string
- Narrative is displayed in the CLI after combat
- At least 5 unique narrative templates per action type

## Notes
- Keep narratives short (1–2 sentences) for terminal width
- Use enemy persona name in the text for personalization
- Consider adding a `combatLog` to `player_ships` for history
